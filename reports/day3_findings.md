# Day 3 EDA Findings

1. NAV values showed a long-term upward trend across most mutual fund schemes from 2022–2026.

2. A few mutual fund schemes significantly outperformed others in NAV growth over the observed period.

3. Nippon India Mutual Fund recorded the highest Assets Under Management (AUM) among analysed fund houses.

4. AUM distribution suggests that a small number of fund houses manage a large share of total mutual fund assets.

5. Monthly SIP inflows remained relatively stable with periodic fluctuations across the observed period.

6. Investors in the 26–35 age group contributed the highest participation in mutual fund transactions.

7. Male investors accounted for approximately two-thirds of the investor base.

8. Verified KYC investors represented over 90% of total investors, indicating strong compliance levels.

9. SIP transactions formed the majority of all transaction types, followed by Lumpsum and Redemption transactions.

10. Most mutual fund schemes exhibited low-to-moderate return correlations, indicating diversification opportunities across funds.
